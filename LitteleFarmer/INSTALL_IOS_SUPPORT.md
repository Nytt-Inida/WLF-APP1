## What's Happening

Your iPhone is running iOS 26.2, but Xcode doesn't have the support files for that iOS version yet. This is normal - you just need to download them.

## ‚úÖ Simple Solution (2 Steps)

### Step 1: Click the "Get" Button

1. **In the dropdown menu** you have open, click the blue **"Get"** button
   - It's next to "‚úì üíª iOS 26.2 Not Installed"
   - OR click the "Get" button in the top toolbar (next to "iOS 26.2 Not Installed")

2. **Xcode will start downloading** iOS 26.2 support
   - This is a large download (several GB)
   - It may take 10-30 minutes depending on your internet speed
   - You'll see a progress indicator

### Step 2: Wait for Installation

- The download will happen automatically
- You can continue using your Mac while it downloads
- Xcode will notify you when it's complete
- Once done, your iPhone will appear as available in the device list
